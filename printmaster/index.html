ARCH

Arches slope up to
a peak and then down.

LOOPS

Loops curve around or
upward starting on one
side of the tip of the finger
and ending on the other.

WHORL

Whorls appear as
a circular pattern.

DOUBLE LOOP

Double Loops show loops
going in two directions.

ENGAGE...

  • Hand lotion
  • Clear tape
  • Powder
  • Small brush (like a paintbrush or makeup brush)
  • Black construction paper
  • Magnifying glass

Put a dab of hand lotion
in your palm and rub it
into your hands.
This will make
your fingerprints
show up more easily.

Press your fingertips onto a hard surface,
like a kitchen counter, bathroom sink,
or dinner plate.

Sprinkle a LITTLE powder
over the surface
that you touched.

Use the brush
to GENTLY brush
away the excess powder.
Powder will stick
to your fingerprint
so don't brush too hard.

Now tear off a piece of
clear tape.
Place the tape directly
on top of the fingerprint.
Press it down.

Slowly pull the tape
off the surface.
The powder will stick to it
and preserve the print.

Press the tape onto
the black construction paper.

Now examine with your magnifying glass and
determine what kind of fingerprints you have:
Arches, Loops, Whorls, or Double Loops?

Everybody leaves fingerprints,
see if you can find some more.

Dip the brush in powder and
lightly dust countertops or sinks
and lift all the existing prints you can find.
Place on the construction paper.

Use your magnifying glass and compare
the prints and see if you can identify yours!

If you want to go further, repeat the
fingerprinting process with
everyone in your family.
See if you can identify their
prints in your collection.
