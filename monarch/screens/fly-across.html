• Monarchs come from eggs
    which hatch caterpillars who feed
    on milkweed plants.
  • Monarchs prefer warm climates.
    If it's dry they can survive freezing
    temperatures, but if they get wet and
    the temperature drops they will die.
  • Adult Monarchs feed on flower nectar
    by sucking it through a tube
    called the proboscis.
  • North American Monarchs are the
    only butterflies that migrate such
    a long distance - up to 3,000 miles!
  • Monarchs fly between
    12 and 25 miles per hour.
  • Monarchs who live west of the
    Rocky Mountains migrate to
    southern California in winter,
    while those who live east of
    the Rockies head to Mexico.
